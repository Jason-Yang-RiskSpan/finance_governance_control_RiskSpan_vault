# SR 11-7: Guidance on Model Risk Management

**Primary Agency:** Federal Reserve (Fed) & OCC
**Relevance to AI:** This 2011 document is the "gold standard" through which U.S. banking regulators view machine learning and AI.

## Three Pillars of Model Governance

### 1. Model Development, Implementation, and Use
*   **Conceptual Soundness:** The model's design must be logically consistent and mathematically sound.
*   **Documentation:** Every aspect of the model must be documented so an outside party can understand and replicate it.
*   **Performance Testing:** Robust testing against historical data (backtesting) and hypothetical "what-if" scenarios.

### 2. Independent Model Validation
*   **The "Second Set of Eyes":** A party independent of the developers must evaluate the model's integrity.
*   **Validation Scope:** Must check data quality, theory, and outcomes.
*   **Ongoing Review:** Models must be re-validated periodically or whenever a significant change occurs.

### 3. Governance and Oversight
*   **Board Accountability:** The Board of Directors is ultimately responsible for model risk.
*   **Model Inventory:** The bank must maintain a complete list of all models in use, their risk levels, and their last validation date.
*   **Risk Appetite:** The bank must define how much risk it is willing to accept from automated decisioning.
