# Third-Party AI Risk Management

**Guidance:** 2023 Interagency Guidance on Third-Party Relationships
**Primary Agencies:** Fed, FDIC, OCC

## Core Principle: No Outsourcing of Responsibility

A bank remains responsible for the safety and soundness of any AI model or data it uses, regardless of whether it was built in-house or purchased from a fintech vendor.

### Key Risk Areas for AI Vendors
1.  **Transparency:** Can the vendor explain the model's logic and data sources?
2.  **Data Security:** How is sensitive customer data (PII) protected when sent to the vendor?
3.  **Governance Maturity:** Does the vendor follow its own model risk management lifecycle?

### Due Diligence Requirements
*   **Audit Rights:** The bank must have the right to audit the vendor's model validation reports.
*   **SLA and Drift Monitoring:** Contracts should include performance requirements and notification when model performance "drifts" outside agreed ranges.
*   **Dependency Risks:** Understanding where the vendor's model comes from (e.g., is it built on an open-source model like Llama or a proprietary model like GPT-4?).
