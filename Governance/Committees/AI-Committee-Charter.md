# AI Committee Charter (V1.0)

**Goal:** Establish a collaborative, cross-functional body to oversee AI strategy, governance, and solution development for [Company Name].

---

## 🏛️ 1. Purpose & Objectives
The AI Committee is responsible for:
- **Strategic Direction:** Aligning AI initiatives with company-wide goals and the "Control Plane" architecture.
- **Governance & Oversight:** Ensuring every AI agent adheres to established [[Internal/Control-Plane/Handwritten-Rules-Policy|Handwritten Rules]] and regulatory compliance.
- **Collaborative Solutioning:** Sharing unique use cases, best practices, and innovative techniques across departments.
- **Risk Management:** Mitigating AI-related risks such as model drift, hallucination, and PII leakage.

## 👥 2. Membership & Roles
The committee consists of a select group of key contributors:
- **Dan / Pat:** Strategic Oversight & Infrastructure Lead.
- **Jason:** AI Strategy & Observability Lead.
- **Honghua / Jungmo:** Technical Implementation & Support.

### Roles:
- **Chair (Dan):** Sets the agenda and leads the monthly meetings.
- **Secretary (Jason):** Documents decisions, tracks JIRA improvement loops, and prepares the "RS Verify" reports.
- **Technical Leads:** Provide expertise on code repository, backend infrastructure, and model validation.

## ⚖️ 3. Key Responsibilities
1. **Agent Approval:** Every AI agent must receive "Sign-Off" from the committee before deployment.
2. **Policy Review:** Monthly review and iteration of the [[Internal/Control-Plane/Handwritten-Rules-Policy|Handwritten Rules & Policy]].
3. **Audit Oversight:** Reviewing the results of the [[Governance/Audits/Digital-Presence-21-Questions|30-day Digital Presence Audit]].
4. **Issue Resolution:** Addressing any guardrail violations or significant model drift flagged by the "Observability Agent."

## 📅 4. Meeting Schedule
- **Frequency:** Monthly (with ad-hoc "Emergency" sessions for critical violations).
- **Format:** In-person or virtual, including a review of the "RS Verify" monthly report.
